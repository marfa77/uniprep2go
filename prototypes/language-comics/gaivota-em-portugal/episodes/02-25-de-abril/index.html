The Carnation Revolution

Before dawn on 25 April 1974, a song on the radio told Portugal the long night of dictatorship was ending.

  • When: 25 April 1974
  • Where: Lisboa — then across Portugal
  • Who: MFA officers and civilians; a nearly bloodless transition
  • Symbol: red carnations placed in rifle barrels
  • After: the Estado Novo dictatorship fell; democracy began

In one day, fear met flowers. Portugal woke to freedom — and never forgot the scent of April.

The comic that follows is an artistic interpretation. It follows two fictional siblings; Gaivota watches. No graphic violence.

3. Cultural note

25 de Abril is Portugal’s democracy day. The red carnation became the symbol of a nearly bloodless revolution that ended the Estado Novo. Every year, Portuguese people still remember the morning the flowers met the rifles.
