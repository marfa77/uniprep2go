The Portuguese Republic

On 5 October 1910, Lisbon woke to a new flag — the Portuguese Republic was proclaimed.

  • When: 5 October 1910
  • Where: Lisbon (and the country follows)
  • Who: republicans, navy units, civilians vs the monarchy of Manuel II
  • How: uprising, civic proclamation; the king leaves into exile
  • After: First Republic begins — new symbols, fragile hopes

A day of barricades and balconies remade the regime. The comic follows two civilians at street level — care, fear, and the moment a green-red flag rises over the same wind.

The comic that follows is an artistic interpretation. It follows two fictional civilians in Lisbon; Gaivota watches. No graphic violence.

Cultural note

On 5 October 1910 the Portuguese Republic was proclaimed in Lisbon after an uprising that ended the monarchy of Manuel II. New national symbols followed; politics remained fragile. The comic stays at street level — a nurse, a friend, a flag, and a city learning a new name.
