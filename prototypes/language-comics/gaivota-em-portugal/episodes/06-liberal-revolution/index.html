The Liberal Revolution

In 1820, Porto rose first — demanding a constitution while the king was still in Brazil.

  • When: August–September 1820 (Porto uprising; movement spreads)
  • Where: Porto, then across Portugal
  • Who: officers, merchants, liberals vs absolutist order; João VI still in Brazil
  • How: pronouncements, assemblies, press, pressure for Cortes and a constitution
  • After: a constitutional path opens; the king returns; politics change

Porto’s voice mattered: print shops, militia, and public oaths pushed Portugal toward written law. The comic follows two siblings at the hinge of that first liberal morning.

The comic that follows is an artistic interpretation. It follows two fictional siblings in Porto; Gaivota watches. No graphic violence.

Cultural note

In 1820 Porto sparked Portugal’s Liberal Revolution: officers, merchants, and citizens pressed for a constitution and Cortes while King João VI was still in Brazil. Print and public oaths mattered as much as muskets. The comic stays human-scale — a sister who prints, a brother who swears, and a city that asked for written law.
