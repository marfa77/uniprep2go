The Treaty of Windsor

In 1386 Portugal and England sealed an alliance at Windsor — still remembered as one of the world’s oldest continuous treaties.

  • When: 1386
  • Where: Windsor (England)
  • Who: Portuguese and English courts after Aljubarrota’s shadow
  • How: diplomacy, seals, mutual defence promises
  • After: a long Anglo-Portuguese friendship begins on parchment

Not a battle — a corridor, a hall, and a signature. Two young attendants watch an alliance that outlives them.

The comic that follows is an artistic interpretation. Human-scale court diplomacy; no battle gore.

Cultural note

The Treaty of Windsor (1386) bound Portugal and England in mutual alliance soon after Aljubarrota. It is often cited among the oldest continuous treaties still invoked in diplomacy. The comic watches the parchment hour through two attendants — not kings in combat.
